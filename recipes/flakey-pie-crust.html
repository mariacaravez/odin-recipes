Flakey Pie Crust

Description

It can be used to make both sweet and savory recipes and is an absolute cinch to prepare!

Ingredients

  • 2 cup + 4 Tbsp flour
  • 1 Tsp salt
  • 2/3 cup of canola oil
  • 4 Tbsp ice water
  • Wax or parchment paper
  • Crust cover or foil paper

Steps

  1. Combine flour and salt in a medium bowl.
  2. Add canola oil to flour mix and stir about 30 times. Tip: DO NOT OVER MIX!
  3. Pour in your 4 tablespoons of ice water.
  4. Stir about 20 more times. Remember: Don't overwork it.
  5. Get two large sheets of parchment paper, big enough to cover your pie dish.
  6. Place your dough inbetween sheets and roll out with pin.
  7. Use your dish face down as a reference and ensure you have about an extra inch of dough all around.
  8. Remove top layer of parchment paper.
  9. Place pie dish face down on top of dough.
  10. Slip hand under bottom parchment paper and flip dough onto pie dish. If crust crumbles or cracks, don't worry! Just patch things up together with your fingertip.
  11. Remove parchment paper and press dough into dish.
  12. Trim off any excess pieces or use them to patch up any gaps. Tip: If you have any leftover dough, you can create cutout decorations for the edges or the center.
  13. Get creative here. There are many ways you can have your crust look beautiful, but I find that a simple pinch works with this crust the best. It is a very flaky crust, so it doesn't want to be overworked.
  14. Refrigerate your crust until ready to use.
